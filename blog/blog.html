Back to Fiction: What I'm Reading This Summer

Why I'm diving back into literature and how it's helping me think differently about economics, data, and creativity.

After years of focusing on technical reading — algorithms, optimization, macroeconomic models — I decided to reintroduce fiction into my life. And it’s been transformational.

Why Fiction Matters

“Fiction is the lie through which we tell the truth.” – Albert Camus

Reading literature sharpens empathy, imagination, and insight — skills that data alone can’t fully teach. My recent reads include *The Unbearable Lightness of Being* and *The Remains of the Day*, both of which challenged my perspective on memory and identity.

What I Learned

  • Fiction offers analogies for abstract models.
  • It develops narrative intuition, useful in data storytelling.
  • It offers a break from constant optimization.

If you’re in a technical field, take a break. Pick up a novel. Your brain — and your soul — will thank you.
